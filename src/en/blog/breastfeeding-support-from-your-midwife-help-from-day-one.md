Breastfeeding is one of the best ways to nourish your newborn — and at the same time, one of the most common challenges new mothers face in the early weeks after birth. As a midwife, I'm here to support you through it.

## What Does Breastfeeding Support Include?

Breastfeeding support covers practical and emotional guidance around feeding. This includes:

- **Latch and positioning:** A correct latch protects your nipples from soreness and ensures your baby is getting enough milk.
- **Milk supply:** Together we can assess whether your milk supply meets your baby's needs, and what steps you can take if you feel you're producing too little.
- **Common challenges:** Sore nipples, engorgement, or the feeling that your baby is not satisfied — many of these difficulties can be resolved with the right support.
- **Weaning:** If you decide to stop breastfeeding, I can guide you through that process as well.

## What Does Health Insurance Cover?

Breastfeeding consultations provided by a midwife are a covered service under Germany's statutory health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenversicherung / GKV), in accordance with § 134a SGB V. If you are publicly insured, you are entitled to breastfeeding support from your midwife — either as a home visit or at the practice.

Billing is handled directly with your health insurer. You do not pay out of pocket.

> **Note:** Exact conditions may vary depending on your insurer. If you are privately insured, please check your policy or contact your insurer directly.

## What Does a Session Look Like?

At the first appointment, I take time to understand your situation: How is feeding going? How does it feel for you? How is your baby behaving?

We then observe a feeding together, and I give you specific, practical advice — tailored to you and your baby.

## When Should You Reach Out?

Please contact me early if:

- Breastfeeding is painful
- Your baby is frequently unsettled and difficult to calm
- You feel your baby is not getting enough milk
- Your breasts feel hard or look red or inflamed

Acting early is always better than waiting. Most breastfeeding difficulties can be resolved — with the right support at the right time.
