Postnatal Care

The first weeks after birth are intense — beautiful and exhausting in equal measure. As your midwife, I visit you at home, support your recovery, help with breastfeeding, and guide you through the early weeks with your newborn.

Postnatal care is divided into two phases: the early postnatal period (days 1–10 of your baby's life) and the late postnatal period (day 11 through to week 12). Both phases are covered as statutory entitlements under § 134a SGB V.

Early Postnatal Period (Days 1–10)

In the first ten days of your baby's life, I am there for you intensively. I come to your home daily — or, where your situation allows, we can connect via video. Typical topics during this time include:

  • Checking your wound healing after birth (perineal tear or caesarean section)
  • Monitoring uterine involution
  • Examining your newborn (weight, umbilical cord, skin, reflexes)
  • Breastfeeding support and latch assistance
  • Support with bottle feeding or pumping if needed
  • Guidance on newborn care, sleep, and settling

Up to 20 contacts

Within the first 10 days of your baby's life.

Home visits

I come to you — especially important in the first days.

Video contact

Possible as an additional contact on the same day.

During the first three days and at your first home visit after discharge from hospital, appointments can be longer if needed.

Late Postnatal Period (Day 11 – Week 12)

In the late postnatal period, the focus shifts increasingly to your own recovery, your relationship with your baby, and settling into family life. I continue to be there as long as you need support. Topics include:

  • Your ongoing wellbeing and physical recovery
  • Continued breastfeeding support and any dietary adjustments
  • Your baby's development and wellbeing
  • Early signs of postnatal depression or emotional strain
  • Preparing for follow-up appointments with your GP, gynaecologist, and paediatrician

Up to 16 contact days

Until the 12th week of your baby's life.

Flexible format

Home visit, practice visit, or video consultation.

Covered by your health insurance

Postnatal midwife care is a statutory entitlement for GKV members under § 134a SGB V. As a GKV-insured person, you are entitled to the services described above within the agreed limits — with no personal contribution required.

Services that exceed the contractually agreed scope can be arranged as a self-pay service (IGeL) — always agreed in writing before the appointment takes place.

Breastfeeding support after week 12

If you still have breastfeeding or feeding questions after week 12, there is a separate insurance-covered service available for that. Learn more about breastfeeding support →

In an emergency

For non-life-threatening medical concerns: 116 117 (out-of-hours GP service). For life-threatening emergencies: call 112 immediately.
