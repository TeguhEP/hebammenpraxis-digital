Pregnancy Care

Being pregnant in Germany — especially far from family — can feel overwhelming. I'm here to accompany you throughout your pregnancy: answering your questions, carrying out routine check-ups, and being there when you need someone who understands.

"Every pregnancy is unique. Some go smoothly; others come with many questions. Whatever your situation, I'm here to support you — from small worries at night to your regular check-ups."

— Raisa Shofia, B.Sc., Midwife (Hebamme)

Flexible ways to meet

I know that getting around during pregnancy isn't always easy. That's why I offer several ways for us to meet, depending on what works best for you:

Home visit

I come to you — no need to travel if you'd rather stay home.

At my practice

In-person consultations and examinations at my practice.

Video consultation

Face-to-face via screen — comfortable from your own sofa.

Phone call

For urgent questions that need a quick answer.

The number of appointments during pregnancy is not strictly fixed — we adapt to your needs and situation.

Routine antenatal check-ups

I carry out antenatal check-ups in line with the German Mutterschaftsrichtlinie (national maternity care guidelines), recording everything directly in your Mutterpass (maternity record). This includes:

  • Blood pressure, weight, and fundal height measurements
  • Listening to the baby's heartbeat
  • Urine testing
  • Monitoring your baby's position and development
  • Discussing any symptoms or concerns you have

These check-ups are covered by your statutory health insurance (GKV), both during a normal pregnancy and when referred by your doctor for specific indications.

Choosing where to give birth

If you're considering a home birth (Hausgeburt), a birth centre (Geburtshaus), or a hospital birth with a personal midwife (Begleit-Beleggeburt), we need to have an in-depth conversation before week 38 of your pregnancy.

Together we'll look at what needs to be prepared, what's medically suitable for your situation, and how to plan a safe birth that feels right for you.

This consultation is covered by insurance and is an administrative requirement for planning a birth outside of a conventional hospital.

Breastfeeding preparation during pregnancy

Breastfeeding is natural — but it can help to prepare. During pregnancy, we can have one dedicated session to discuss things that are personal to you and wouldn't come up in a group class:

  • Assessment of your breasts and nipples for any particular considerations
  • Previous breastfeeding difficulties you may have experienced
  • Guidance on collecting colostrum before birth
  • Any medical or anatomical questions about breastfeeding

This individual breastfeeding preparation session is available once during pregnancy and is covered by your health insurance.

What's covered by insurance?

All services described on this page are statutory entitlements for GKV members under § 134a SGB V. There is no out-of-pocket cost within the agreed scope.

If you'd like additional services beyond what's covered, these can be arranged as a self-pay service (IGeL) — always agreed in writing before the appointment takes place.

In an emergency

For non-life-threatening medical concerns: 116 117 (out-of-hours GP service). For life-threatening emergencies: call 112 immediately.
