If you're covered by Germany's statutory health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenversicherung / GKV), you have a legal right to midwife care — at no cost to you. Many families, especially expats navigating the German healthcare system for the first time, don't know this. Here's a clear breakdown of what's included.

## What Does "Kassenleistung" Mean?

Kassenleistung (literally: "insurance service") refers to any healthcare service that your statutory health insurance covers in full. You pay nothing out of pocket — your midwife bills the insurance directly.

Midwife services in Germany are regulated under **§ 134a SGB V**, the law that defines what GKV members are entitled to.

## What Midwife Services Are Covered?

### Pregnancy Care
Your midwife can support you throughout your pregnancy — alongside or instead of your gynaecologist for certain consultations. This includes check-ins, basic monitoring, and advice on physical complaints during pregnancy.

### Birth Preparation
GKV members are entitled to reimbursement for one birth preparation course per pregnancy. Many insurers also contribute to a partner course — check directly with your insurer.

### Postnatal Care
After birth, your midwife visits you at home. She checks on your recovery, monitors your baby's health, and supports you through questions and challenges in the early weeks — all covered by your insurance.

### Breastfeeding Support
Struggling with breastfeeding? Consultations with your midwife are a covered service. There is no charge to you.

## Do I Need a Referral?

**No.** You can contact a midwife directly — no referral from a doctor is needed.

> **Important tip:** Find your midwife as early as possible in your pregnancy. Many midwives have limited availability, especially from the second trimester onwards.

## How It Works

1. Contact the midwife of your choice
2. Sign a care agreement
3. Your midwife bills your insurance directly — no paperwork for you
