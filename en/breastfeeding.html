Breastfeeding Support

Breastfeeding is natural — but it isn't always easy. I accompany you from preparation during pregnancy, through your baby's first weeks, and beyond, whenever difficulties arise.

"Many mothers stop breastfeeding not because they can't — but because no one was there at the hardest moment. I want to be the person who shows up then."

— Raisa Shofia, B.Sc., Midwife (Hebamme)

Breastfeeding support begins in pregnancy and continues for as long as you need it. It is available in three distinct phases, each with different coverage — all reimbursable by statutory health insurance (GKV) under § 134a SGB V.

Phase 1

Breastfeeding Preparation in Pregnancy

Before your baby arrives, we can use one dedicated session to prepare for breastfeeding in a way that's tailored specifically to you. This is ideal for questions that can't be addressed in a group setting. Topics may include:

  • Assessment of your breasts and nipples for any particular considerations
  • Previous breastfeeding difficulties or experiences
  • Guidance on how to collect colostrum before birth
  • Any medical or anatomical questions about breastfeeding

This individual preparation session is available once during pregnancy and is covered by your health insurance.

Phase 2

Breastfeeding Support During the Postnatal Period

(Days 1–Week 12)

During the postnatal period, breastfeeding support is fully integrated into every midwife visit. I help you from the very first latch until nursing becomes something that feels natural. We'll look at:

  • Positioning and latch technique to prevent pain
  • Making sure your baby is getting enough milk — reading hunger and satiety cues
  • Treating sore, cracked, or swollen nipples
  • Prevention and management of blocked ducts or mastitis (Milchstau)
  • Guidance on using a breast pump when needed
  • Emotional support for mothers who are finding breastfeeding difficult or are considering stopping

This is included within postnatal care (§ 134a SGB V) and fully covered by insurance. Learn more about postnatal care →

Phase 3

Breastfeeding & Feeding Difficulties After Week 12

From week 13 until the end of the weaning phase

Even after the postnatal period ends, GKV-insured mothers are entitled to midwife support for breastfeeding and infant feeding difficulties. This service is available:

  • For breastfeeding difficulties — from week 13 until you and your baby decide to wean
  • For infant feeding problems (e.g. difficulty feeding, insufficient weight gain) — until the end of your baby's 9th month of life

Up to 8 contact days

After the postnatal period, for breastfeeding and feeding problems.

Flexible format

Home visit, practice visit, or video consultation.

This service is billed directly to your health insurance. No doctor's referral is required.

Covered by insurance or self-pay?

All services described on this page are statutory entitlements for GKV members under § 134a SGB V. Within the agreed limits, there is no cost to you.

If you'd like additional consultations beyond the covered scope, these can be arranged as a self-pay service (IGeL) — always agreed in writing before the session takes place.

In an emergency

For non-life-threatening medical concerns: 116 117 (out-of-hours GP service). For life-threatening emergencies: call 112 immediately.
