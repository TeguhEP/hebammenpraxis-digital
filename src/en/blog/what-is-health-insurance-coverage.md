If you're pregnant in Germany and covered by statutory health insurance (gesetzliche Krankenversicherung / GKV), you have a legal right to professional midwife care — at no cost to you. Many expats don't know this, or find the German system confusing. This article breaks it down clearly.

## What Does "Kassenleistung" Mean?

Kassenleistung literally means "insurance service" — a healthcare service that your statutory health insurer covers in full. You pay nothing out of pocket. Your midwife bills the insurance directly.

The legal foundation is **§ 134a of Germany's Social Code V (Sozialgesetzbuch V / SGB V)**, which defines the midwife services that GKV members are entitled to.

> **Please note:** The services below apply to members of Germany's statutory health insurance (GKV). If you have private health insurance (private Krankenversicherung / PKV), please check your individual policy, as coverage varies.

## What Services Are Covered?

### Pregnancy Care
Your midwife can accompany you throughout your pregnancy — as a complement to your gynaecologist (Frauenarzt/Gynäkologe), not as a replacement. This includes consultations, support for physical complaints, and a space to ask questions you might not have time for at a busy clinic.

### Birth Preparation Course (Geburtsvorbereitungskurs)
GKV members are entitled to reimbursement for one birth preparation course per pregnancy. Some insurers also contribute to a partner course — check directly with your insurer, as this varies between providers.

### Postnatal Home Visits (Wochenbettbetreuung)
After birth, your midwife comes to you — at home. She monitors your physical recovery, checks on your baby's wellbeing, helps with newborn care, and is there for you during the moments that can feel most overwhelming. All covered by your insurance.

### Breastfeeding Support (Stillberatung)
Breastfeeding difficulties are extremely common — and not a sign that something is wrong. Consultations with your midwife for breastfeeding support are a covered service. There is no charge.

## Do I Need a Referral?

**No.** You can contact a midwife directly — no referral (Überweisung) from a doctor is required.

> **A practical tip:** Start looking for your midwife as early as possible — ideally in your first trimester. Experienced midwives fill up quickly, especially for postnatal care. Don't wait until the third trimester.

## How It Works — Step by Step

1. Contact the midwife of your choice
2. Sign a care agreement (Betreuungsvereinbarung)
3. Your midwife handles billing directly with your insurer — no forms for you to submit
